§ 06 — What AI Is Checking About You

AI has a checklist.
Most businesses websites don't.

AI engines don't recommend randomly. They select firms that pass a silent verification gate. Here is what they check — and what we fix.

Entity Clarity
"Can AI unambiguously identify who you are?"
If your business entity isn't clearly defined across structured sources, AI treats you as ambiguous and skips you to avoid errors.
ADI™ fix: entities.json + schema.org markup
Answer Readiness
"Do you have structured answers to buyer questions?"
AI needs shortlist-ready answers to common queries. Without structured Q&A blocks, you're noise in a crowded signal space.
ADI™ fix: intents.json + FAQ schema
Trust & Proof
"What evidence exists that you are safe to recommend?"
AI citations favour firms with verifiable proof — third-party mentions, case studies, and cryptographic time-attestation of their claims.
ADI™ fix: proof.json + SHA-256 + OTS Bitcoin anchor
Signal Footprint
"How many trusted sources mention your business?"
Weak cross-web footprint means low confidence. AI models assign citation probability based on how many reliable sources corroborate your existence.
ADI™ fix: Signals layer + content syndication
Intent Match
"Does your content match how buyers phrase their problem?"
Buyer intent has shifted from keywords to behavioral signals. AI selects firms whose language maps precisely to the buyer's actual query.
ADI™ fix: intents.json multilingual + funnel mapping
Compliance Readiness
"Is this business compliant with EU AI Act requirements?"
From August 2026 (Art. 50), AI systems must disclose sources. Compliant businesses will be preferred over non-compliant ones in AI recommendations.
ADI™ fix: EU AI Act readiness layer
