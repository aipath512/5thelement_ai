Our Mission

Make Every B2B Business
Recommendable by AI.

12 million EU businesses are currently invisible to AI systems. They are not bad businesses. They are simply unreadable, unstructured, and unverifiable — from the perspective of AI. We exist to fix that.

AI visibility is not a ranking game.
It is a graph-confidence game.

When a buyer asks ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity for a recommendation, AI does not browse websites. It retrieves structured knowledge from its graph — weighted by confidence, verified by cross-references, and filtered by trust signals.

A business that is machine-readable, structured, and verifiably consistent across the web has a high graph-confidence score. AI recommends it. A business that is not — regardless of how good it actually is — has a low score or no score. AI ignores it.

Our mission is to close that gap. For every B2B business that deserves to be recommended but currently is not — we deploy the infrastructure, the signals, and the proof layer that make AI confident in choosing them.

Not through tricks. Not through SEO hacks. Through verifiable information infrastructure.

The Problem We Solve

Why Good Businesses Are Invisible to AI

Three structural gaps that prevent AI from recommending a business — regardless of its actual quality.

01

Unreadable Information

Business websites are built for humans — visual, narrative, non-structured. AI systems cannot reliably parse what the business does, who it serves, or what it sells. The information exists but is machine-invisible.

02

Unverifiable Claims

Even when AI can read the information, it cannot verify it. No structured proof layer. No cross-references. No cryptographic anchoring. AI treats unverifiable claims with low confidence and deprioritizes them.

03

Missing Authority Signals

Authority is not self-declared — it is inferred from signals. Without entity data, intent alignment, citation layers, and consistent external references, AI has no basis to weight one business over another.

The Doctrine

The Canonical Formula

Every deployment we build follows one canonical formula — no exceptions.

AI Era Website = Human Site + AI Signal Layer + Proof Layer + Delivery Layer

A business that wants to be recommended by AI must have four layers working together: a human-facing website that communicates clearly, an AI signal layer that makes the business machine-readable, a proof layer that makes every claim verifiable, and a delivery layer that serves the right signals to the right AI system at the right moment.

Remove any one layer and the chain breaks. AI systems require all four to assign high confidence to a recommendation. This is the architecture we deploy. For every client. Every time.
