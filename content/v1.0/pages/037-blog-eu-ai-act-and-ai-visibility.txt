── WHAT IS THE EU AI ACT ──

The EU AI Act is the first comprehensive AI regulation in the world.

Official Name:

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

Purpose:

Create rules for the development, deployment, and use of AI systems.

The regulation introduces:

Transparency

Governance

Risk Management

Human Oversight

Documentation

Accountability

── WHY IT MATTERS ──

AI is becoming part of business operations.

The EU wants organizations to:

Understand their AI usage.

Document their AI systems.

Manage risks.

Protect users.

Provide transparency.

The EU AI Act affects:

AI Providers

AI Deployers

Importers

Distributors

Businesses using AI

Public organizations

── KEY DATES ──

August 1, 2024

EU AI Act entered into force.

---

February 2, 2025

Rules on prohibited AI practices begin.

---

August 2, 2025

General Purpose AI obligations begin.

---

August 2, 2026

High-risk AI obligations begin.

Major milestone for many organizations.

---

August 2, 2027

Additional provisions become fully applicable.

── AI VISIBILITY AND THE EU AI ACT ──

AI Visibility is not legal compliance.

Recommendable™

The EU AI Act focuses on:

Governance

These areas overlap.

But they are not the same thing.

── IMPORTANT RULE ──

Use:

"Supports Readiness"

Do NOT use:

"Certified"

"Compliant"

"Approved"

unless there is legal evidence supporting the claim.

This is called the Epistemic Rule.

── HUMAN OVERSIGHT ──

Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) means:

Humans review important decisions.

Humans approve deployments.

Humans validate outputs.

Human oversight is a major principle of the EU AI Act.
