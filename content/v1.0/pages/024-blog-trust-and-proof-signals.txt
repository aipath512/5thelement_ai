── WHAT THEY ARE ──

Trust and Proof Signals are the 61 data points that help AI determine:

Can this business be trusted?

Can its claims be verified?

Should it be recommended?

This is the highest-weight category in AI recommendation systems.

Trust builds credibility.

Proof builds verifiability.

Together they create confidence.

── TRUST vs PROOF ──

Trust Signals

External validation.

Other people and organizations reference the business.

Examples:

Citations

Reviews

Professional memberships

Awards

Case studies

Directories

---

Proof Signals

Cryptographic and documentary evidence.

Examples:

SHA-256 hashes

OpenTimestamps anchors

Bitcoin verification

Proof files

Governance declarations

Verification records

── EPISTEMIC DISCIPLINE RULE ──

Every claim must be classified as:

self_declared

publicly_verifiable

independently_certified

Rule:

Use:

"supports readiness"

Never use:

"certified"

"compliant"

"satisfies"

unless legally proven.

── WHY THIS CATEGORY MATTERS MOST ──

Identity tells AI who you are.

Intent tells AI what you do.

Trust and Proof tell AI whether it should recommend you.

Recommendation depends heavily on this category.

── FAQ ──

1. Which signal category has the highest weight?

Trust and Proof.

2. What is the most important proof signal?

Verified OpenTimestamps status.

3. What is the most important trust signal?

External citations.

4. Why are case studies important?

They provide evidence of outcomes.

5. Can AI recommend a business without proof?

Sometimes.

But confidence and recommendation frequency remain lower.
