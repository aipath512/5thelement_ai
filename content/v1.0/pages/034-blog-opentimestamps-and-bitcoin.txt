── WHAT IS OPENTIMESTAMPS ──

OpenTimestamps (OTS) is a protocol that proves a document existed at a specific point in time.

It uses:

SHA-256

Bitcoin

Cryptographic verification

No central authority is required.

No trust in a company is required.

Verification is public.

Verification is independent.

── THE PROBLEM ──

Anyone can claim:

"We invented this."

"We published this first."

"We created this methodology."

But proving it later is difficult.

OpenTimestamps solves this problem.

It creates independent proof.

── HOW IT WORKS ──

STEP 1

Create a document.

Example:

Research paper

Methodology

Innovation

Policy

Framework

---

STEP 2

Generate SHA-256 hash.

Example:

31aa7a56...

The hash becomes the document fingerprint.

Any change creates a different hash.

---

STEP 3

Submit hash to OpenTimestamps.

The hash joins many other hashes.

---

STEP 4

OTS creates a Merkle Tree.

Hashes are grouped together.

---

STEP 5

Bitcoin records proof.

The Merkle Root becomes anchored to Bitcoin.

---

STEP 6

Receive .ots file.

Example:

document.pdf.ots

This file contains verification information.

── WHAT IS SHA-256 ──

SHA-256 is a cryptographic hashing algorithm.

Input:

Document

Output:

Unique fingerprint

Example:

Document A

↓

31aa7a56...

If one character changes:

↓

Different hash

This makes tampering detectable.

── WHAT IS THE BITCOIN BLOCKCHAIN ──

Bitcoin stores immutable records.

Once anchored:

Nobody can modify the timestamp.

Nobody can rewrite history.

Nobody can remove the proof.

This makes Bitcoin useful as a proof system.

── AI BENEFITS ──

Proof of methodology existence.

Proof of innovation existence.

Proof of governance documents.

Proof of authority claims.

AI systems can verify evidence.

AI confidence increases.
