The Problem

Most Businesses Have No Idea How AI Sees Them

Most businesses investing in digital presence have never asked the question that now determines whether a buyer finds them or their competitor: what does ChatGPT say when asked to recommend a business like mine?

The answer is almost always one of three things. Either AI cannot find the business at all. Or AI finds it but describes it incorrectly. Or AI finds it, describes it partially, but recommends a competitor instead — because that competitor has stronger authority signals.

AI AUDIT™ answers this question precisely, systematically, and verifiably. Not with an opinion. With a score.

The Audit Framework

Six Layers. Weighted Contributions.

Each layer is scored independently and combined into a composite AI Readiness Score. Weighting reflects the impact of each layer on AI recommendation probability.

01
15% weight

Entity Identity

Can AI correctly identify this business? Legal name, trading name, category, location, founding date, and legal identifiers — consistent across all sources and present in the knowledge graph.

02
20% weight

Service Clarity

Can AI accurately describe what this business sells? Services named, categorised, and described in machine-readable format. Each service mapped to a buyer intent with target client defined.

03
15% weight

External Verification

Can AI confirm claims from sources it did not generate itself? sameAs references active, certifications schema-marked, company registry data consistent with website data.

04
20% weight

Signal Architecture

Are the 167 atomic AI signals deployed? Which are present, missing, inconsistent, or unverifiable? Is the signal layer served from the edge? Are AI crawlers permitted?

05
20% weight

Authority Layer

Does AI treat this business as a credible, authoritative source? External citations, case studies with schema markup, sector directory presence, cryptographic proof layer, third-party verification.

06
10% weight

Recommendation Readiness

Does AI name this business when buyers ask for a recommendation? On which AI systems does it appear? For which queries? With what description accuracy? How does it compare to top competitors?

The Score

AI Readiness Score — 0 to 100

A composite number calculated from all six layers. Each band represents a distinct AI visibility and authority state.

0 — 39

Invisible or Misrepresented

AI cannot find the business or describes it incorrectly. Immediate action required across all six layers before any marketing investment will have AI visibility impact.

40 — 69

Partially Visible

AI can find the business and partially describe it. Not yet recommendable. Competitors with higher scores will capture AI recommendations for shared target queries.

70 — 89

Visible and Verifiable

AI can find, understand, and verify the business. May still lack authority signals required for consistent recommendation. Focus shifts to TRUST LAYER™ and PROOF LAYER™.

90 — 100

Recommendable™

AI names this business in recommendation responses for target buyer queries. Consistent across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and Perplexity. Verified by eu-ai-audit.eu.

The Gap Matrix

Every Gap. Ranked by Impact.

Every identified gap is entered into the Gap Matrix — structured, ranked, and referenced to the product that closes it.

What Each Gap Entry Contains

Gap ID

Unique identifier for tracking and cross-referencing across audit cycles.

Layer

Which of the six audit layers the gap belongs to.

Impact Score

How much closing this gap improves the overall AI Readiness Score.

Effort Estimate

Low, medium, or high implementation effort.

Fix Reference

Which product or action closes this specific gap.

Before/After Simulation

Before State

Current status of the gap — missing, inconsistent, or unverifiable.

After State

Projected status once the gap is closed — present, consistent, and verified.

Score Projection

Exact projected improvement to AI Readiness Score if this gap is closed.

Priority Rank

Impact divided by effort — highest value gaps surfaced first.

machine-readable

gap-matrix.json consumed directly by AI READY™ and SIGNAL COMPILER™.

Signal Assessment

167 Signals. Four Status Tags.

Every signal is assessed individually and tagged. The assessment file is consumed directly by AI SIGNALS™ for systematic deployment.

🟢

Present

Signal exists, is correctly structured, and is accessible to AI systems.

🔴

Missing

Signal does not exist in any accessible location.

🟡

Inconsistent

Signal exists but conflicts with data in another source.

⚪

Unverifiable

Signal exists but cannot be confirmed from an external source.

The Process

Five Steps. 24 Hours.

01

Submission

Submit domain, primary service category, target geography, and top three competitors. No website access required. No credentials. No login.

02

Automated Assessment

All six layers assessed automatically. Entity data retrieved from public sources. Signal files checked. Schema validated. External references cross-referenced. AI systems queried for recommendation frequency.

03

Human Review

Every automated finding reviewed by a human analyst before the report is finalised. HITL checkpoint — no gap is reported without human confirmation.

04

Delivery

Full output package — score, gap matrix, signal assessment, competitor benchmark, and audit report — delivered within 24 hours of submission.

05

Debrief

Optional 30-minute debrief call. Analyst walks through findings, answers questions, and recommends highest-impact next actions.
