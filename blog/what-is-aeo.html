What Is AEO? Answer Engine Optimisation Explained for B2B

📅 June 10, 2026 🏢 AiVenture S.R.L. ⏱ 8 min read 🔗 Glossary: AEO

The Short Answer

AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation — is the practice of structuring business information so that AI systems such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, and Perplexity can find, understand, and cite it when answering buyer queries.

It is not a replacement for SEO. It is a parallel discipline that targets a fundamentally different type of information retrieval — one that is growing faster than traditional search and already changing how B2B buyers discover, evaluate, and select suppliers.

AEO is to AI systems what SEO is to search engines. The signals required are different. The infrastructure is different. The results are different. But the business impact is the same — or greater.

Why AEO Exists — The Shift to Answer Engines

Search engines return a list of ranked pages. Answer engines return a synthesised answer. The buyer no longer needs to click through ten pages and evaluate them independently — the AI does the evaluation and delivers a recommendation.

This changes everything about how business information must be structured. A page that ranks #1 on Google but is invisible to AI systems will lose buyers who ask ChatGPT for a recommendation. Both can be true simultaneously — and increasingly are.

For B2B buyers, the shift is accelerating. Procurement research, supplier discovery, and competitive evaluation are all moving toward AI-assisted workflows. The businesses that appear in AI-generated answers are capturing this demand. The businesses that do not are invisible to it.

The Five Stages of AEO — From Findable to Recommendable

AEO is not a single action. It is a progressive maturity model with five distinct stages. Each stage builds on the previous and adds new capability to how AI systems process and recommend the business.

  • Stage 1 — Findable™: AI can locate the business entity in the knowledge graph. Basic entity data exists in machine-readable format.
  • Stage 2 — Understandable™: AI can correctly read what the business sells, who it serves, and what problem it solves.
  • Stage 3 — Verifiable™: AI can confirm claims from external sources. sameAs references, certifications, and schema are consistent.
  • Stage 4 — Trustable™: AI treats the business as a credible authority. Citations, proof layer, and governance are deployed.
  • Stage 5 — Recommendable™: AI names the business when buyers ask for a recommendation in its market.

The 167 AI Signals That Power AEO

AEO is implemented through 167 atomic machine-readable signals — structured data points that AI systems read when they assess a business. These signals fall into three categories:

  • Identity and Entity (48 signals) — who the business is, what it does, where it operates
  • Trust and Proof (61 signals) — verifiable evidence that claims are accurate
  • Intent and Governance (58 signals) — buyer intent alignment and governance declarations

The Key AEO Files Every Business Needs

AEO requires a set of machine-readable files deployed at your domain root. These are the files AI systems look for first when they try to understand a business:

  • ai.json — primary entity definition file
  • llms.txt — LLM instruction and permission file
  • entities.json — complete entity relationship graph
  • intents.json — buyer intent registry
  • robots.txt — updated to permit all AI crawlers

The ADI™ Rule — Signals → Files. Never Files → Signals.

The canonical AEO pipeline runs RAW → FACT → ENTITY → RELATIONSHIP → SIGNAL → FILE. Signal values are determined from verified business knowledge first. Files are generated from those signal values. Reversing this order produces inconsistent data that fragments your knowledge graph entry.
