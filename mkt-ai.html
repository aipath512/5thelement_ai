§ 07 — Questions

Everything you
need to know.

How is MKT-AI different from a traditional marketing agency?
A traditional agency optimises for clicks, impressions, and Google rankings — metrics that matter less when buyers are using AI to research and shortlist vendors. MKT-AI optimises for AI citation frequency — making your business the answer AI engines give when buyers search for your category. Every piece of content we produce is structured, entity-explicit, and proof-anchored so AI models can cite it with confidence.
Do I need to already have AI-Ready deployed?
AI-Ready (the ADI™ signal layer) and MKT-AI work best together — the signal layer gives AI engines the foundation they need to find and trust your business, while MKT-AI builds the content and citation network on top. However, MKT-AI services like content strategy, production, and intent mapping can begin before the ADI™ layer is deployed. We recommend running a free AI audit first so we can prioritise correctly.
How long before I see results in AI answers?
First signals go live within 24 hours of deployment. AI engines begin updating their citation indexes within 24–72 hours. You will see measurable citation frequency within 30 days. Authority compounds progressively — month 3 results are significantly stronger than month 1. Unlike paid advertising, every signal we build persists and strengthens over time.
Is the content produced by AI or by humans?
Both — strategically combined. Our content strategy and entity architecture is built by our team. Content production uses AI tools under human editorial control to maintain quality, consistency and brand voice. Every piece is reviewed, structured with schema markup, and anchored to your proof layer before publication. We never publish raw AI output.
Can MKT-AI work alongside my existing marketing team?
Yes. MKT-AI is designed to augment existing teams, not replace them. We can operate as a specialist AI marketing layer on top of your existing agency or in-house team — providing the AI-specific infrastructure (signal layer, schema markup, intent mapping, proof anchoring) that most marketing teams don't have the expertise to build themselves.
